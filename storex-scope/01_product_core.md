# Storex ‚Äî Product Core

## One-liner
Storex is an AI sales person for each business.  
It actively sells, advises, and converts customers 24/7 across social channels.

---

## What Storex DOES

- Engages customers in real time (comments & messages)
- Understands products, stock, pricing, and context
- Recommends the right product at the right time
- Guides conversations toward checkout
- Advises merchants how to sell more effectively
- Provides built-in payments & BNPL with no external contracts
- Automates delivery coordination and customer updates
- Learns from customer behavior to improve sales

---

## What Storex is NOT

- Not a passive chatbot
- Not just automation rules
- Not a CRM or marketplace
- Not a manual-heavy SaaS tool
- Not a website builder
- Not a separate payment processor

---

## Target Users

### Merchant Owner (Decision Maker)
- **Pain**: Losing sales due to slow response times
- **Goal**: Increase sales without hiring more staff
- **Storex Value**: AI handles conversations 24/7, converts customers automatically

### Staff / Operator
- **Pain**: Overwhelmed by repetitive customer questions
- **Goal**: Focus on fulfillment and edge cases
- **Storex Value**: AI handles 90% of customer interactions, staff steps in only when needed

### End Customer (Facebook / Instagram User)
- **Pain**: Waiting hours for merchant replies
- **Goal**: Buy fast with confidence
- **Storex Value**: Instant responses, product recommendations, easy checkout

### 4. Trust Through Transparency

## Risk Mitigation

### Business Risks

**Risk**: Merchants don't trust AI with customers  
**Mitigation**: AI transparency, merchant oversight, gradual automation

**Risk**: Payment/BNPL fraud  
**Mitigation**: DAN verification, transaction monitoring, risk scoring

**Risk**: Poor AI responses damage brand  
**Mitigation**: AI training, merchant review queue, manual override

### Technical Risks

**Risk**: AI token costs too high  
**Mitigation**: Token-based pricing, usage optimization, hybrid AI/rules

**Risk**: Social platform API changes  
**Mitigation**: Multi-platform strategy, direct message fallback

**Risk**: Scaling infrastructure  
**Mitigation**: Cloud-native architecture, horizontal scaling
